# FAQ

### &#x20;<a href="#why-do-we-need-lavaland-in-the-first-place" id="why-do-we-need-lavaland-in-the-first-place"></a>

Dollar-pegged stablecoins have become an essential part of crypto due to their lack of volatility as compared to tokens such as Bitcoin and Ether. Users are comfortable with transacting using stablecoins knowing that they hold the same amount of purchasing power today vs. tomorrow. But this is a fallacy. The dollar is controlled by the US government and the Federal Reserve. _This means a depreciation of dollar also means a depreciation of these stablecoins._

Lavaland aims to solve this by creating a non-pegged stablecoin called **LAVA**. By focusing on supply growth rather than price appreciation, Lavaland hopes that **LAVA** can function as a currency that is able to hold its purchasing power regardless of market volatility.

### &#x20;<a href="#is-time-a-stable-coin" id="is-time-a-stable-coin"></a>

**No, LAVA is not a stablecoin**. Rather, **LAVA** aspires to become an algorithmic reserve currency backed by other decentralized assets. Similar to the idea of the gold standard, **LAVA** provides free-floating value its users can always fall back on, simply because of the fractional treasury reserves **LAVA** draws its intrinsic value from.

### &#x20;<a href="#time-is-backed-not-pegged-1" id="time-is-backed-not-pegged-1"></a>

Each **LAVA** is backed by 1 **DAI**, not pegged to it. Because the treasury backs every **LAVA** with at least 1 **DAI**, the protocol would buy back and burn **LAVA** when it trades below 1 **DAI**. This has the effect of pushing **LAVA** price back up to 1 **DAI**. **LAVA** could always trade above 1 **DAI** because there is no upper limit imposed by the protocol. Think pegged == 1, while backed >= 1.

You might say that the **LAVA** floor price or intrinsic value is 1 **DAI**. We believe that the actual price will always be 1 DAI + premium, but in the end that is up to the market to decide.

### &#x20;<a href="#how-does-it-work" id="how-does-it-work"></a>

At a high level, Lavaland consists of its protocol managed treasury, protocol owned liquidity, bond mechanism (minting), and high staking rewards that are designed to control supply expansion.

Bonding in the "Mint" page generates profit for the protocol, and the treasury uses the profit to mint **LAVA** and distribute them to stakers. With LP bond, the protocol is able to accumulate liquidity to ensure the system stability.

### &#x20;<a href="#why-is-pcv-important" id="why-is-pcv-important"></a>

As the protocol controls the funds in its treasury, **LAVA** can only be minted or burned by the protocol. This also guarantees that the protocol can always back 1 **LAVA** with 1 **DAI**. You can easily define the risk of your investment because you can be confident that the protocol will indefinitely buy **LAVA** below 1 **DAI** with the treasury assets until no one is left to sell. You can't trust the FED but you can trust the code.

As the protocol accumulates more PCV, more runway is guaranteed for the stakers. This means the stakers can be confident that the current staking APY can be sustained for a longer term because more funds are available in the treasury.

### &#x20;<a href="#why-is-the-market-price-of-time-so-volatile" id="why-is-the-market-price-of-time-so-volatile"></a>

It is extremely important to understand how early in development the Lavaland protocol is. A large amount of discussion has centered around the current price and expected a stable value moving forward. The reality is that these characteristics are not yet determined. The network is currently tuned for expansion of **LAVA** supply, which when paired with the staking, minting, and yield mechanics of Lavaland, result in a fair amount of volatility.

LAVA could trade at a very high price because the market is ready to pay a hefty premium to capture a percentage of the current market capitalization. However, the price of **LAVA** could also drop to a large degree if the market sentiment turns bearish. We would expect significant price volatility during our growth phase so please **do your own research** whether this project suits your goals.

### &#x20;<a href="#what-is-a-rebase" id="what-is-a-rebase"></a>

Rebase is a mechanism by which your staked **LAVA** balance increases automatically. When new **LAVA** are minted by the protocol, a large portion of it goes to the stakers. Because stakers only see staked **LAVA** balance instead of **LAVA** the protocol utilizes the rebase mechanism to increase the staked **LAVA** balance so that 1 staked **LAVA** (**sLava**) is always redeemable for 1 **LAVA**.

### &#x20;<a href="#what-is-reward-yield" id="what-is-reward-yield"></a>

Reward yield is the percentage by which your staked **LAVA** balance increases on the next epoch. It is also known as _rebase rate_. You can find this number on the Lavaland staking page.

### &#x20;<a href="#what-is-apy" id="what-is-apy"></a>

**APY** stands for annual percentage yield. It measures the real rate of return on your principal by taking into account the effect of compounding interest. In the case of Lavaland, your staked **LAVA** represents your principal, and the compound interest is added periodically on every epoch (8 hours) thanks to the rebase mechanism.

One interesting fact about **APY** is that your balance will grow not linearly but exponentially over time! Assuming a daily compound interest of 2%, if you start with a balance of 1 **LAVA** on day 1, after a year, your balance will grow to about 1377.

### &#x20;<a href="#how-is-the-apy-calculated" id="how-is-the-apy-calculated"></a>

The APY is calculated from the reward yield (a.k.a rebase rate) using the following equation:

APY=(1+rewardYield)1095APY = ( 1 + rewardYield )^{1095}

It raises to the power of 1095 because a rebase happens 3 times daily. Consider there are 365 days in a year, this would give a rebase frequency of 365 \* 3 = 1095.

Reward yield is determined by the following equation:

rewardYield=LAVAdistributed/LAVAtotalStakedrewardYield = LAVA\_{distributed} / LAVA \_{totalStaked}

The number of LAVA distributed to the staking contract is calculated from LAVA total supply using the following equation:

Note that the reward rate is subject to change by the protocol.

### &#x20;<a href="#why-does-the-price-of-time-become-irrelevant-in-long-term" id="why-does-the-price-of-time-become-irrelevant-in-long-term"></a>

As illustrated above, your **LAVA** balance will grow exponentially over time thanks to the power of compounding. Let's say you buy a **LAVA** for $400 now and the market decides that in 1 year time, the intrinsic value of **LAVA** will be $2. Assuming a daily compound interest rate of 2%, your balance would grow to about 1377 **LAVA** by the end of the year, which is worth around $2754. That is a cool $2354 profit! By now, you should understand that you are paying a premium for **LAVA** now in exchange for a long-term benefit. Thus, you should have a long time horizon to allow your **LAVA** balance to grow exponentially and make this a worthwhile investment.

You can track your rebase rewards by calculating the increase in your staked **LAVA** balance.

2\. After staking for some time, if you want to determine by how much your balance has increased, check the Current Index value again. Let's call this the End Index.

3\. By dividing the End Index by Start Index, you would get the ratio by which your staked **LAVA** balance has increased.
